Each Hadamard gate splits one qubit's amplitude equally across |0⟩ and |1⟩. Apply H to all 3 qubits and the probability spreads equally across all 8 states — the wavefunction now "encodes" all of them.

Entanglement links qubits
A CNOT gate entangles two qubits — measuring one instantly determines the other, no matter how far apart. The system can no longer be described as independent qubits. It becomes one inseparable joint state.
After entanglement, |Ψ⟩ cannot be written as qubit A ⊗ qubit B ⊗ qubit C.
Only |000⟩ and |111⟩ survive — perfectly correlated pairs.
Interference sculpts the probabilities
This is the engine of Grover's Algorithm. A mathematical Oracle secretly flagged our target `|110⟩` by turning its amplitude negative. A diffusion operator then uses that negative amplitude as a mirror—causing wrong answers to cancel out (destructive interference) while forcing `|110⟩` to rise and dominate (constructive interference).
Measurement collapses everything
The wavefunction collapses to one outcome. Because we engineered the amplitudes, the right answer is overwhelmingly likely. You get one result — but almost certainly the correct one.
